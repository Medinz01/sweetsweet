SYSTEM REQUIRMENT SPECIFICATIONS 
 
Customizable SAAS platform with LLM-Powered Analytics 
 
PURPOSE OF THE SYSTEM  
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) establishes the functional and non-
functional specifications for a SaaS solution designed for micro, home-based, and small B2C 
sellers. The solution empowers users to create personalized storefronts, efficiently manage 
inventory and orders, and leverage AI-driven business intelligence. All critical data is 
maintained in a PostgreSQL database, with actionable recommendations generated by a 
Large Language Model (LLM), accessed securely via the Model Context Protocol (MCP). 
PROBLEMS IN THE EXISTING SYSTEM:  
Most SaaS e-commerce platforms offer only basic, template-driven customization for seller 
homepages and product displays. Deep brand personalization or unique workflows are 
often restricted, requiring costly upgrades or development 
Sellers have limited ownership of the data they generate. With SaaS, the platform provider 
maintains control; if policies change or accounts get suspended, access to essential business 
data can be compromised 
With restricted customization comes bland storefronts—many sellers end up with nearly 
identical shopfronts, making it difficult to stand out or implement unique customer 
experiences 
Default dashboards mostly offer basic, historical reporting—lacking real-time insights, 
anomaly detection, or cross-metric correlation. This leaves sellers blind to subtle issues or 
unable to proactively spot and act on opportunities 
Integrating with legacy systems, external tools, or AI/LLM components is often challenging. 
Such integrations can be costly, inflexible, and sometimes impossible to implement on top 
of SaaS-locked platforms

SOLUTION OF THESE PROBLEMS  
Provides fully customizable, seller-specific homepages with drag-and-drop or flexible layout 
options allowing unique brand presentation rather than fixed templates. 
Stores all key business data securely in your PostgreSQL/MySQL database owned and 
accessible by the seller; integration via MCP ensures controlled, auditable AI access. 
Integrates a Large Language Model via MCP to analyze live sales and inventory data, 
providing real-time, actionable insights, trend detection and anomaly alerts. 
Uses Model Context Protocol (MCP) for a standardized, secure AI-to-database interface 
allowing seamless integration of LLM-driven intelligence with minimal custom plumbing. 
Employs secure LLM access only through MCP, ensuring privacy and compliance; provides 
sellers simple natural-language insights without complex coding or AI management needs. 
 
SCOPE OF THE PROJECT  
This project delivers a Software as a Service (SaaS) solution enabling micro, home-based, 
and small B2C sellers to create, customize and manage personalized online storefronts. 
Sellers can easily design their homepage, arrange products, banners, promotions 
without technical expertise. All critical business data—products, orders, inventory and 
customer details—is securely maintained in a PostgreSQL or MySQL relational database. 
This ensures strong data integrity, consistency and seller ownership of their business 
information. The platform integrates a Large Language Model (LLM) through the Model 
Context Protocol (MCP), providing sellers with real-time, context-aware analytics, actionable 
recommendations, and trend detection based on live transactional data. Sellers manage 
product catalogs, inventory and orders through an intuitive dashboard, benefiting from 
automated insights and alerts driving better business decisions.

STUDY OF THE SYSTEM : 
The current e-commerce landscape for micro, home-based, and small B2C sellers is 
dominated by SaaS platforms that, while easy to set up, present significant limitations. 
Existing systems often force sellers into rigid, template-based storefronts, which restricts 
brand differentiation and offers minimal customization. Furthermore, sellers typically lack 
true ownership and control over their business data, face escalating hidden costs with 
growth, and are provided with basic, retrospective analytics that offer little predictive or 
actionable value. 
Integrating advanced technologies like AI for business intelligence is often complex, 
expensive, and outside the technical reach of these non-technical users. The primary 
problem is a market gap: a lack of an affordable, scalable, and intelligent e-commerce 
solution that provides deep customization, data ownership and powerful, real-time analytics 
to empower small sellers.

User Characteristics: 
Sellers: The main users of the platform. They are micro, small, or home-based 
business owners with minimal technical expertise. They will use the system to 
manage their entire online business—from storefront design to order fulfillment and 
performance analysis. 
Customers: The end-users who visit the sellers' storefronts to browse and purchase 
products. The system provides them with a simple, secure, and seamless shopping 
experience. 
 
Proposed System and Objectives 
To address these challenges, this project proposes the development of a multi-tenant SaaS 
e-commerce solution specifically designed for micro and home-based sellers. The system 
will provide sellers with the tools to build and manage a fully personalized online storefront, 
moving beyond the limitations of generic templates. 
The core architecture is built on a robust relational databases, which serves as a centralized 
and secure repository for all seller data, including products, inventory, orders, and customer 
information. This ensures data integrity and provides sellers with ownership of their 
business intelligence. 
The key innovation of the proposed system is the integration of a Large Language Model 
(LLM) via the Model Context Protocol (MCP). This allows the platform to securely connect 
to the live PostgreSQL/MySQL data and deliver real-time, AI-driven insights. Sellers receive 
actionable recommendations—such as product bundling suggestions, sales trend alerts, and 
inventory forecasts—directly on their dashboard in plain, easy-to-understand language. 
The primary objectives of the system are: 
Provide an intuitive, no-code interface for creating a unique and highly personalized brand 
presence. 
 Leverage AI to transform raw sales data into strategic insights that drive business growth. 
Give sellers control over their business data with a secure, reliable backend. 
Streamline product, inventory, and order management through a single, user-friendly 
dashboard.

INPUT / OUTPUT   
Inputs: 
• 
Seller enters their user ID and password to log in. 
• 
Seller customizes their storefront layout, uploads banners, and arranges products. 
• 
Seller adds or edits product details, including name, price, description, images, and 
inventory count. 
• 
Seller updates the status of a customer's order (e.g., Confirmed, Shipped, Delivered). 
• 
Seller asks a natural language question to the AI for business insights (e.g., "What 
were my top-selling items last week?"). 
• 
Customer browses the storefront and adds items to their shopping cart. 
• 
Customer provides their name, contact info, and delivery address during checkout. 
• 
Customer submits a review and a rating for a product they purchased. 
Outputs: 
• 
The system displays the seller's personalized dashboard upon successful login. 
• 
The seller's public-facing storefront is updated instantly to reflect layout changes 
and new products. 
• 
New product information, order details, and customer data are stored securely in 
the PostgreSQL database. 
• 
The system displays the current status of all orders on the seller's dashboard. 
• 
The system displays real-time, AI-generated answers, reports, and actionable 
suggestions to the seller. 
• 
The system displays an order confirmation page to the customer after a successful 
purchase. 
• 
An automated email notification is sent to the customer for order confirmation and 
shipping updates. 
• 
The system displays product reviews and ratings on the relevant product pages. 
PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS: 
Performance is a critical measure of the output and user experience provided by this SaaS 
application. A clear specification of these requirements is essential during the analysis phase 
to ensure the final system is robust, responsive, and meets the operational demands of both 
sellers and their customers. Since the platform is designed to replace inefficient or manual 
existing systems, its performance must be demonstrably superior to justify its adoption. It is 
very difficult to re-engineer performance characteristics once the system has been 
designed, and a platform that does not meet the performance expectations of its users will 
fail to retain them. 
 
 
 


===== PAGE 6 =====

The performance requirements for this system can be stated as follows: 
 
• 
Response Time and Latency: The system must be highly responsive to user 
interactions. Key performance indicators include: 
o Seller dashboard and customer-facing storefront pages should load in under 
3 seconds on a standard internet connection. 
o Database queries for product information or order history must return results 
in under 2 seconds. 
o Real-time actions, such as adding a product to the cart or updating inventory, 
should be reflected in the system almost instantaneously (under 500ms). 
• 
Scalability and Concurrency: The platform must be able to handle a growing number 
of sellers and their customers without degradation in performance. It should support 
hundreds of concurrent user sessions per seller during peak traffic events (e.g., a 
promotional sale) and maintain performance as the total number of sellers on the 
platform grows into the thousands. 
• 
Data Accuracy and Integrity: The system must ensure complete accuracy in all its 
operations. Inventory counts must be updated in real-time with every sale to 
prevent overselling. Financial calculations for order totals, discounts, and seller 
payouts must be precise. All data stored in the PostgreSQL database must maintain 
its integrity. 
• 
AI Insight Performance: The AI-driven agents and the LLM analytics must provide 
timely and relevant insights. 
o Requests for simple, natural language business queries should be answered 
within 5-10 seconds. 
o Automated insights and alerts (e.g., low-stock warnings) must be generated 
and delivered to the seller with minimal delay from the triggering event. 
 
FEASIBILITY REPORT 
A study was conducted to assess the feasibility of the proposed system across three key 
areas: 
• 
Technical Feasibility: The project is technically feasible. It relies on a stack of mature, 
widely-adopted, and well-documented technologies, including PostgreSQL, Node.js, 
React/Next.js, and established cloud hosting platforms. The integration of an LLM via 
the Model Context Protocol (MCP) represents a modern but well-defined 
architectural pattern for securely connecting AI to live data sources. The modular 
design of the system also ensures that components can be developed, tested, and 
deployed independently, reducing complexity and risk. 
• 
Operational Feasibility: The system is designed with a high degree of operational 
feasibility. Its target users—non-technical sellers—are the central focus of the user 
experience. The platform will feature an intuitive dashboard, guided onboarding, 
and automated insights, which minimizes the need for specialized training or

technical skills. By automating complex data analysis and presenting it in a simple 
format, the system ensures that sellers can easily incorporate it into their daily 
operations. 
• 
Economic Feasibility: The project is economically feasible. By utilizing open-source 
technologies and a SaaS subscription model, the initial development costs are 
manageable and the barrier to entry for sellers is low. The platform provides a high-
value proposition by offering enterprise-grade analytics and customization at a 
fraction of the cost of developing a bespoke solution. This creates a strong potential 
for high user adoption and a sustainable revenue model based on recurring monthly 
or annual subscriptions.
